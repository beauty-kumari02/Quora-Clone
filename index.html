NYC always amazes me
.
Jan 25

How does moving to the USA from India feel like?

I spent most of my life in India before I made it to NYC a little over 6 months ago. When I got my L1 visa stamped in Dec’23, I couldn’t constrain my joy. I had always dreamt of working abroad and the opportunity to work in the heart of the world’s most powerful country was nothing short of a miracle for me. When I landed at the JFK airport after completing my longest flight trip, and took a cab on the way down to Jersey City, I was amazed by the infrastructure.

I loved the way cities are designed with road dissecting each other at right angle, keeping a broad path for pedestrians to walk freely. What was a tussle to walk evading multi-modal traffic in India amidst noise and dust, seemed an easy traverse. I could walk miles and miles without feeling vulnerable and tired.

I rented an apartment in a city little farther from the NYC in New Jersey. Living in NJ is a popular choice due to lower taxes and a thriving Indian diaspora making it easy access to Indian food and groceries and also opportunity to make friends who can talk in your native language. It felt incredible to see so many familiar faces when I strolled down the Indian Street in Journal Square.

While public transportation is excellent in terms of connectivity in NYC, it becomes sparse as you move away from the city. Due to lack of local public transport, and exorbitant cab ride cost, if you don’t own a car, it’s makes your life incredibly challenging. I had to travel to Lower Manhattan for work, and the worst part of the journey was using the subway. It is the one of the oldest underground rail network in the world which brings with itself several challenges for MTA, the authority managing the network. Having used the Delhi Metro countless times, I couldn’t help but appreciate the efficiency and modernity of DMRC in comparison.

I really enjoyed the work culture in my office- best part being it’s incredibly flexible. While people don’t gossip so much here in office than in India, I loved how strangers would wave at me. Everyone is easy to approach and ready to help which made me settling into new environment much easier.

When I arrived here in Apr, it was a receding winter but it still felt cold enough for my Indian genes. Having now experienced the temp as low as -18 degrees during this winter, I have now got accustomed to it. What has amazed me is the apartment design here which insulates you almost completely from outside weather. I could stay comfortably in my tees inside even though it snowing and freezing cold outside.

There are times when I miss my friends and family but life is great at getting adapted. While I love peace and quiet here, it also sometimes takes the form of solitude. Another challenge is the healthcare cost. I’ve been fortunate enough not to incur significant expenses but it can create a big hole in your pocket.

I like to have new experiences and explore new cities, so I was able to accept the change easier. Each day brings something new, and while it’s not always easy, the journey has been worth it so far.
